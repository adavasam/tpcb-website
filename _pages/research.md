## Research at TPCB

Chemical biology at the tri-institutional level encompasses an unusually broad range of scientific approaches and biological questions. TPCB faculty pursue original research across multiple scientific themes, united by the use of chemical tools to address fundamental problems in biology.

### Scientific Themes

**Chemical Synthesis and Methodology**  
Faculty develop new synthetic methods and apply them to the construction of complex natural products, designed scaffolds, and chemical probes. The program has particular strength in the total synthesis of bioactive natural products and the development of asymmetric catalysis.

**Structural Biology and Biophysics**  
A major emphasis is placed on understanding the three-dimensional structures of proteins, nucleic acids, and their complexes. Faculty use X-ray crystallography, cryo-electron microscopy, NMR spectroscopy, and computational methods to reveal the molecular basis of biological function.

**Chemical Genetics and Target Identification**  
Faculty develop and apply small molecule probes to dissect cellular signaling pathways, identify new drug targets, and understand protein function in living cells. Activity-based protein profiling, chemoproteomics, and genome-wide chemical genetic screens are among the tools used.

**Chemical Biology of Disease**  
Research across all three institutions is strongly translational, with faculty working on the molecular mechanisms of cancer, infectious disease, neurodegeneration, and metabolic disorders. TPCB students routinely work on projects with direct therapeutic relevance.

**Computational Chemical Biology**  
Faculty develop and apply computational methods—molecular dynamics simulation, machine learning, structure-based drug design, and systems modeling—to problems in chemical biology and drug discovery.

**Natural Products and Metagenomics**  
TPCB has a distinguished tradition in natural product chemistry, including the discovery and biosynthetic study of microbial natural products from environmental and human microbiome sources.

### Core Facilities

TPCB students have access to state-of-the-art core facilities at all three institutions:

| Facility | Location | Capabilities |
|---|---|---|
| NMR Spectroscopy | WCM, Rockefeller | High-field solution and solid-state NMR |
| X-Ray Crystallography | Rockefeller, MSK | In-house diffractometers; access to synchrotron beamlines |
| Cryo-Electron Microscopy | MSK | Single-particle analysis, cryo-tomography |
| Mass Spectrometry / Proteomics | WCM, Rockefeller | LC-MS/MS, native MS, metabolomics |
| High-Throughput Screening | MSK | Automated compound library screening |
| Flow Cytometry | WCM, MSK | FACS sorting, high-content imaging |
| Genomics and Sequencing | All three | Illumina short-read; Oxford Nanopore long-read |

### Seminar Series

The TPCB Chemical Biology Seminar Series brings distinguished speakers from academia and industry to the program throughout the academic year. Seminars alternate between the WCM and Rockefeller campuses and are open to the entire tri-institutional community. See the [news page](/news/) for announcements of upcoming speakers.
