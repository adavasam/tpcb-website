## About TPCB

The Tri-Institutional PhD Program in Chemical Biology (TPCB) is a joint graduate program of [Weill Cornell Medicine](https://www.med.cornell.edu/), [The Rockefeller University](https://www.rockefeller.edu/), and [Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center](https://www.mskcc.org/). Founded in {{ site.data.program.founding_year }}, TPCB trains scientists to use the tools of chemistry to address fundamental problems in biology and medicine.

Students in the program have unparalleled access to three world-class research institutions located within a few blocks of each other on the Upper East Side of Manhattan. This unique arrangement means that TPCB students can work with faculty from any of the three campuses, attend seminars across all three institutions, and benefit from a rich intellectual community spanning chemistry, biology, and medicine.

## Why TPCB?

- **Three institutions, one program**: Access to more than 65 faculty spanning three of the world's leading biomedical research institutions.
- **Exceptional training environment**: Graduates publish an average of {{ site.data.program.avg_publications }} papers during their PhD.
- **New York City**: World-class science in one of the world's great cities.
- **Strong track record**: TPCB graduates go on to careers in academia, industry, and beyond.
