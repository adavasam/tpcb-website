## Program of Study

The TPCB PhD program is designed to train rigorous, creative scientists with deep expertise in chemical biology. The program typically takes five to six years to complete.

### Year 1: Rotations and Coursework

First-year students complete three laboratory rotations, each approximately ten weeks long, before selecting a thesis laboratory. Rotations may be in any participating faculty lab across all three institutions. Students also take a core curriculum in chemical biology that provides a common foundation in organic chemistry, biochemistry, structural biology, and cell biology.

### Years 2–5: Thesis Research

After joining a lab, students complete a qualifying examination, form a thesis advisory committee, and focus on independent dissertation research. The program encourages interdisciplinary projects that span chemistry and biology, and students are expected to develop skills in scientific communication through seminars and journal clubs.

### Core Curriculum

Required coursework includes:
- **Principles of Chemical Biology** — the interface of chemistry and biology, with emphasis on small molecule tools
- **Structural Biology** — structural methods including X-ray crystallography, NMR, and cryo-EM
- **Biochemistry and Cell Biology** — molecular mechanisms of cellular processes
- **Chemical Biology Laboratory** — hands-on techniques in organic synthesis, biochemistry, and chemical biology

### Requirements

- Three laboratory rotations (Year 1)
- Qualifying examination (end of Year 2)
- Thesis advisory committee meetings (annual)
- Annual progress reports
- Dissertation defense and oral examination
