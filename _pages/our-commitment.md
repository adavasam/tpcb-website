## Our Commitment to Students

TPCB is committed to recruiting and supporting a diverse community of scientists and to creating an environment in which every student can thrive. We believe that diversity—of background, perspective, and approach—strengthens science and is essential to the program's mission.

### Mentoring and Support

Every TPCB student is supported by a thesis advisory committee that meets at least annually to assess progress, provide feedback, and help navigate challenges. The program director and program coordinator are available to discuss any concerns, and students are encouraged to seek support early when issues arise.

The program has a formal ombudsperson process for students who wish to raise concerns confidentially, and all three institutions have student affairs offices with counseling and mental health resources.

### Commitment to Diversity

TPCB actively recruits students from underrepresented groups in STEM, including students from racially and ethnically underrepresented backgrounds, first-generation college students, students from low-income backgrounds, and students with disabilities. We partner with the [TPCB Summer Research Program](/summer-program/) to introduce undergraduate students from underrepresented groups to chemical biology research at our three institutions.

### Funding and Financial Security

All admitted students receive:
- **Full tuition coverage** for the duration of the PhD
- **Competitive annual stipend**, adjusted annually
- **Health, dental, and vision insurance**
- **Travel funds** to attend scientific conferences
- **Research funds** for laboratory expenses

Students are not responsible for securing their own funding. The program ensures financial continuity between rotations and thesis labs.
