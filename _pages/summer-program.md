## TPCB Summer Undergraduate Research Program

The TPCB Summer Undergraduate Research Program provides exceptional undergraduate students with the opportunity to conduct independent research in chemical biology laboratories at Weill Cornell Medicine, The Rockefeller University, and Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center.

The 10-week program runs from June through August and is designed for students who are considering graduate school in chemistry, chemical biology, biochemistry, or related fields. Participants are paired with a faculty mentor, carry out a focused research project, and participate in a rich series of professional development activities.

### What the Program Offers

- **Research experience** in a leading chemical biology laboratory
- **Weekly seminars** from TPCB faculty on their current research
- **Professional development workshops** on graduate school applications, scientific communication, and career planning
- **Housing** in New York City for the duration of the program
- **Competitive stipend** to cover living expenses
- **Closing symposium** where participants present their research

### Who Should Apply

The program is open to undergraduate students at any accredited US college or university who:
- Have completed at least two years of college-level chemistry or biochemistry
- Have prior research experience (required or strongly preferred)
- Are US citizens or permanent residents (due to funding requirements)

The program especially encourages applications from students from underrepresented groups in STEM, including students from racially and ethnically underrepresented backgrounds, first-generation college students, and students from low-income backgrounds.

### How to Apply

Applications are accepted in the winter for the following summer. The typical application deadline is **January 15**. Required materials include:
- Two letters of recommendation
- Unofficial transcript
- Personal statement (500 words)
- CV or resume

### Past Participants

Many TPCB Summer Program alumni have gone on to enroll in the TPCB PhD program and other leading graduate programs in chemistry and biology. We are proud of their accomplishments and grateful for the community they represent.
