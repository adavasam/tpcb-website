## External Fellowships

TPCB students are encouraged to apply for external fellowships, which provide additional research funding, career recognition, and independence. Below is a list of fellowships that are particularly relevant for chemical biology PhD students.

### Federal Fellowships

**NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program (GRFP)**  
The NSF GRFP provides three years of fellowship support to outstanding graduate students in STEM fields. Chemical biology students are competitive for Chemistry, Biochemistry, and Molecular and Cellular Biosciences panels. *Deadline: October (Year 1 and Year 2).*

**NIH F31 NRSA Individual Predoctoral Fellowship**  
The F31 provides up to five years of support for predoctoral students pursuing research relevant to NIH mission areas. Students in TPCB labs with NIH-funded research are well-positioned for this fellowship. *Deadline: Multiple cycles per year.*

**DOE CSGF Computational Science Graduate Fellowship**  
For students with a strong computational component to their research. *Deadline: January.*

### Private Fellowships

**Paul & Daisy Soros Fellowship for New Americans**  
For immigrant and child-of-immigrant graduate students. Provides two years of support. *Deadline: November.*

**Hertz Fellowship**  
Highly competitive fellowship for students in the physical and biological sciences, mathematics, or engineering. *Deadline: October.*

**Ford Foundation Predoctoral Fellowship**  
For students committed to diversity in higher education and research, particularly those from underrepresented groups. *Deadline: December.*

**American Chemical Society Division of Medicinal Chemistry Predoctoral Fellowship**  
For students working in medicinal chemistry and chemical biology with relevance to drug discovery.

---
