## Life in New York City

TPCB students live and work on the Upper East Side of Manhattan, one of New York City's most dynamic and historically rich neighborhoods. All three campuses—Weill Cornell Medicine, The Rockefeller University, and Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center—are located within a few blocks of each other along York Avenue, making it easy to collaborate across institutions and participate in the full breadth of tri-institutional scientific life.

### Housing

The program provides assistance in finding housing in New York City. All three institutions offer subsidized graduate student housing options within walking distance of the campuses, and program staff help incoming students navigate the process of finding a place to live before they arrive.

### Cost of Living

TPCB students receive a competitive annual stipend sufficient to cover living expenses in New York City. The program also provides health insurance, tuition coverage, and research funds. Stipend levels are reviewed annually and adjusted to reflect changes in the cost of living.

### New York City

Beyond the lab, New York City offers world-class museums, restaurants, music, theater, and parks. Students regularly take advantage of:

- **Central Park**, just a few blocks from campus, for running, cycling, and outdoor relaxation
- The **Metropolitan Museum of Art**, the **Museum of Natural History**, and dozens of other world-class museums
- A restaurant scene encompassing virtually every cuisine in the world
- Vibrant neighborhoods from Brooklyn to the Bronx, each with its own distinct character

### Student Life

TPCB students organize regular social events including department retreats, journal clubs, intramural sports, and cultural outings. The small cohort size fosters close friendships across different labs and institutions, and many students cite the community as one of the program's greatest strengths.
