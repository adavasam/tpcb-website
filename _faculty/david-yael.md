The David lab investigates the chemical biology of chromatin and epigenetic regulation using a combination of chemical synthesis, biochemistry, and cell biology. The lab develops novel chemical tools—including semisynthetic histones, proximity labeling reagents, and covalent inhibitors—to decode the complex language of histone modifications and their role in gene regulation and disease. Current research focuses on understanding how histone modification crosstalk coordinates transcriptional programs in cancer.
