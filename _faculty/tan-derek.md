The Tan lab uses organic synthesis and chemical genetics to study biological problems, particularly in the areas of natural product-inspired drug discovery and protein function. The lab develops new synthetic methods and applies them to the construction of complex bioactive molecules, including natural products and designed scaffolds with unprecedented structural features. A major focus is identifying and validating new therapeutic targets in cancer and infectious disease using small molecule probes.
